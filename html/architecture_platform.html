Platform Architecture

All tools, services, tasks, and engagements under one... Canopy

The overarching goal of Canopy is to house all tools, content, and services under one roof so that our tax professionals can more easily get their jobs done.

Tools, Tasks, Services, Engagements

The lego bricks of Canopy can be broken down to one of three things: Tasks, Tools, Services, Engagements.

  • Tools are a wide range of components used by tax professionals to accomplish their work.
  • Tasks have been created to allow tax professionals to assign small portions of work to other team members, individually or for collaboration within services.
  • Services have the goal of grouping tools and/or tasks in different combinations to allow our tax professionals delivery to their clients.
  • Engagements are then used by tax professionals to organize everything and bill their client for services, tasks, and tools.

Workspaces and Feedback Views

There are alot of views and pages inside Canopy. However, any view can be broken down to one of two user interfaces: Workspace or Feedback Boards.

  • Workspaces are places within the application where the tax professional can create, edit and delete content. Workspaces can be one specific page or cards or a series of pages and cards grouped together.
  • Feedback views and cards contain content for tax professionals to consume. These can also be pages or a series of pages, views and cards put together to tell a story.

Templates

A template is a very powerful tool used to help our practitioners be more efficient. While a template is simply a tool, it is very important to the structure of Canopy. A task or service template will be the vehicles to creating instances of tasks and services for a practitioners' clients. For most content types there will exist a template, either created by Canopy or a custom template created and managed by the practitioner.

Global Add

Practitioners and tax professionals can get very distracted as they are working with phone calls, texts, questions, and interruptions by team members and clients. To mitigate this distraction in Canopy we have created the "global add menu" located in the platform navigation bar, where the practitioner can create content from anywhere inside Canopy while not losing their place on the thing they are currently working on.

The Restaurant Metaphor

We often look for real world concepts and processes that are familiar to our tax professionals and use them as metaphors to explain and create workflows and processes. A great metaphor for how Canopy works and behaves is to liken it how most restaurants function.

  • Canopy tools are like cooking utensils, tables and point of sale.
  • A tasks can be compared to tasks done in a restaurant like a server taking an order or generating a bill, a chef preparing an entree, a sous chef preparing a side dish, a buser cleaning the tables and a manager verifying a plate before it is sent out.
  • A services are simply menu items, plates and side dishes to be prepared for diners.
  • An engagement is compared to the experience a diner has from walking in the door to paying the check.
