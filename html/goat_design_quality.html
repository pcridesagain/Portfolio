Design Quality

Preventing design debt and staying cohesive.

To prevent design debt and maintain a cohesive, consistent user experience across all tools, services and features of our platform. Weâ€™ve designed a couple checklists to be used to insure that the design and experience is built and released complete. These checklists should not only apply to the design being assessed but also its integration into the existing application and surroundings.

Checklists

The lists below should be used by the designer during creation of wireframes, mockups, and prototypes and should be reviewed before any design is handed off to engineering. Quality Assurance should also use the list after build to ensure the quality of design has been passed onto the production application. Any iterative changes made during the build phase should also be reviewed to maintain quality and mockups may also need to be updated to reflect them.

Is it complete?

Are proper hover states present? Are important hover actions still accessible?

Are there at least 3 levels of information architecture present or considered? (Primary, Secondary, Tertiary nav)

Are there proper success, error, or informational messages present?

Are empty states needed? Are they designed to standard?

Are there loaders present?

Are all CRUD (Create, Rename, Update, Delete) operations present?

Is it up to standard?

Does the design follow GOAT standards and patterns? If not, is there an acceptable reason to deviate?

Does the design follow the voice and tone guidelines?

Do elements have clear affordance for how they can be interacted with?

Are the design elements aligned and laid out correctly on a soft 8 pixel grid?

Does the design properly use visual hierarchy and call to actions?
