Grammar

The best communication start with clear, consistent readability.

Consistent and clear communication is very important to the overall experience of the practitioner. Even if we meet all the design quality standards and principles, improper grammar will quickly destroy all of it.

Capitalization

Sentence case

  • Use sentence case for content and paragraph headings.
  • Capitalise the first letter of the first word in a heading – like you would in a sentence. Proper nouns also have a capital.

Title case

  • Use title case for titles, headings, and buttons inside the product for better scannability when wayfinding through the application.
  • Capitalize the first word in the title.
  • Capitalize the last word in the title.
  • Capitalize the important words in the title; adjectives, adverbs, nouns, pronouns, subordinating conjunctions, and verbs.
  • Don’t capitalize; articles (a, an, the), coordinating conjunctions (and, but), and short prepositions (at, by, from).

Exclamation Points

  • Exclamation marks often cheapen sentences. Use words to convey emotion rather than punctuation.
  • There are a few exceptions, but if you’re debating it, lean of the side of no exclamation marks.

Ellipses

  • Ellipses have specific uses that rarely come up. Like exclamation marks, they often cheapen your sentence.
  • Steer clear of using ellipses to trail off a sentence.

Ampersands

Only two acceptable uses

  • Connecting two names within a title i.e. Barton & Gaff Accounting.
  • Following the IRS precedent.

Adverbs

  • Adverbs are often the easy way of saying something rather than the best way. For example, instead of saying something is really fast, you could say it’s instant.
  • Adverbs to watch out for: Really, very.
