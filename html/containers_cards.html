Cards

Cards are used to group and isolate like content and interactions on the artboard. There are four types of cards: L1, L2, L3 and DT (dark theme).

Level 1

L1 cards should be used as first layer on top of the canvas. They have a 1px corner radius, and 1px border and light dropshadow.

DT (Dark Theme)

DT cards can be used to add more affordance or visual contrast when used alongside other L1 and L2 cards. They are also used to convey to the user feedback or show content from another area of Canopy. DT cards have a 1px corner radius.

Level 2

L2 cards are the second layer on top of the canvas. They should be used on top of an L1 card. They have a 5px corner radius, and 1px border and a light drop shadow.

Level 3

L3 cards are the third layer on top of the canvas. L3 cards should be used as the top layer of any view. They have a 8px corner radius, and 1px border. They are usually accompanied by a dark overlay below the card.

Guidelines

  • All cards should have 24px padding
  • All L1, L2, L3 cards should be Bumble (#FFFFFF)
    with a Silver (#E9E9E9)
    border
  • DT cards are (#3C464D)
  • Cards should never be used within other cards to break up content - instead use wells to contain content within cards
  • Never use a DT card by itself. DT cards should always be used in conjunction with L1 cards

Best Practices

  • L1 cards should be used to contain most content.
  • L2 cards are generally used as popovers and menu items.
  • L3 cards should be used for modals and overlays.
